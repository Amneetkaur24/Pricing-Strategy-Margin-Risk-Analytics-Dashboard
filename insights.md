# Key Insights — Pricing Margin Risk Analytics

## 1. Medium Discounts Provide the Best Revenue–Margin Balance

Orders with **medium discount levels** generate the most balanced outcome between revenue growth and profitability.
While discounts stimulate demand, moderate discounting maintains healthier margins compared to aggressive price reductions.

## 2. High Discounts Increase Margin Risk

Orders with **high discounts contribute disproportionately to high-risk margin segments**.
Although revenue may increase temporarily, the gross margin percentage drops significantly, creating long-term profitability risk.

## 3. Majority of Orders Operate in Medium Risk Zone

The margin risk distribution shows:

* Healthy Risk: 62 orders
* Medium Risk: 426 orders
* High Risk: 32 orders

This indicates that most transactions operate within **moderate margin tolerance**, but a small portion of heavily discounted orders drives elevated risk.

## 4. Category Contribution to Revenue Is Uneven

Revenue contribution varies significantly across product categories.
Certain categories generate higher revenue but also display increased sensitivity to discounting, requiring careful pricing controls.

## 5. Discount Strategy Should Be Data-Guided

The analysis suggests that **controlled discounting strategies outperform aggressive promotions**.
Moderate discounts preserve margins while maintaining stable sales volumes.

---

# Executive Recommendation

Organizations should avoid deep discount campaigns unless supported by strategic objectives such as inventory clearance or customer acquisition.

A **data-driven pricing strategy focused on moderate discount ranges** can help sustain revenue growth while protecting overall profitability.
